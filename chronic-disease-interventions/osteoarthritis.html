Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Structural progression is essentially universal over 10+ years without intervention; pain fluctuates but functional decline is the expected trajectory; no disease-modifying therapy exists as of 2024
Best-intervention remission
No cure or disease modification available; total joint replacement (TJR) achieves 85-90% patient satisfaction and near-elimination of pain but is not disease reversal; weight loss of >10% reduces pain scores ~50% and slows structural progression; exercise therapy comparable to NSAIDs for pain relief
Gap size
Large for pain management vs disease modification (disease modification doesn't exist); moderate for functional outcomes (exercise and weight loss underutilized vs medication)
Primary barrier
biological + practice
OARSI formally petitioned the FDA in 2016 to recognize OA as a serious disease with no cure and no disease-modifying interventions — confirmed in FDA's criteria. Practice gap: NSAIDs have been first-line for >100 years despite long-term evidence of joint damage acceleration and GI/CV risk; exercise consistently outperforms medication in functional outcomes but is rarely 'prescribed'
