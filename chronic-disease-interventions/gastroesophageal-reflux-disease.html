Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Symptom recurrence in 70-80% within 6 months of PPI discontinuation in erosive GERD; highly variable in non-erosive disease; ~20% of GERD is nonerosive, where acid suppression is less effective
Best-intervention remission
Weight loss of >10% reduces symptoms in 65-81% of obese patients; laparoscopic anti-reflux surgery (Nissen fundoplication) achieves long-term symptom control in 85-92% and eliminates daily PPI need in majority; bariatric surgery in obese patients near-eliminates GERD
Gap size
Large for lifestyle/surgical approaches vs standard chronic PPI use
Primary barrier
framing/practice
AGA 2022 guidelines explicitly recommend PPI de-escalation or discontinuation in patients without erosive esophagitis or Barrett's — yet 84 million monthly Medicare PPI claims in 2019 suggest massive overprescription; most patients are never offered surgical or intensive lifestyle options
