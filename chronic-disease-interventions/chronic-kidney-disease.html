Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
GFR decline averages 2-5 mL/min/year on standard care (ACE/ARB + blood pressure control); progression to end-stage renal disease (ESRD) in ~20-30% at 10 years for moderate CKD; reversal essentially impossible once structural fibrosis established
Best-intervention remission
SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) reduce progression to ESRD by 30-35% in CKD trials (EMPA-KIDNEY, DAPA-CKD); finerenone (non-steroidal MRA) adds 15-20% additional protection; combination slows but does not reverse disease
Gap size
Moderate — SGLT2 inhibitors represent a genuine paradigm shift but are dramatically underused
Primary barrier
practice/access
SGLT2 inhibitors have robust evidence for CKD protection regardless of diabetes status (EMPA-KIDNEY 2023) but prescribing rates in non-diabetic CKD remain very low; ACE/ARB as sole strategy (established in 1990s-2000s) still dominant despite the new data
