Research Tracker / Chronic Disease Interventions / Heart Failure

Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Annual mortality ~22% on standard care; only 5-11% of HFrEF patients achieve normalisation of ejection fraction (HFrecEF) with GDMT; HFpEF has no disease-modifying therapy
Best-intervention remission
GDMT (ARNI + beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2i) reduces CV mortality by 38% and hospitalization by 25% vs historical standard; reverse cardiac remodelling (EF improvement >10%) in 40-50% of HFrEF with full quadruple therapy; cardiac resynchronisation therapy (CRT) improves EF in 70% of LBBB patients
Gap size
Large — quadruple GDMT dramatically underused; <25% of eligible HFrEF patients receive all four pillars
Primary barrier
practice/access
Full quadruple therapy guideline-endorsed since 2021 but implementation is poor; SGLT2i in HF still widely under-prescribed despite Class 1A recommendation; ARNI (sacubitril/valsartan) uptake slow due to cost
