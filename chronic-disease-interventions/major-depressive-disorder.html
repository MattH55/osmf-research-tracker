Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Relapse in 19-45% within 1 year of antidepressant discontinuation after remission; relapse risk ratio (off-medication vs on) falls from 3.69x at 2 months to 1.34x at 5 years
Best-intervention remission
55-80% remain relapse-free at 1 year after well-managed discontinuation; psychotherapy (CBT) achieves comparable remission with better durability; psilocybin-assisted therapy showing 29-71% response at 3 months in treatment-resistant cases
Gap size
Moderate; narrows substantially over time for maintenance medication question
Primary barrier
practice
Guidelines push long maintenance durations partly because short-term relapse risk is high, but long-term maintenance evidence is weaker than commonly assumed; psychotherapy significantly underutilized relative to medication
