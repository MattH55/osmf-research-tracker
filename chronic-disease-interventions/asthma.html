Disease Overview

Spontaneous remission
26% of childhood-onset asthma in clinical remission by adulthood; <20% adult-onset achieves sustained remission; 65% long-term remission in mild-moderate vs 16% in severe
Best-intervention remission
Biologics (anti-IL-5, anti-IL-4/13, anti-IgE) achieve near-zero exacerbation rates in eligible severe patients; allergen immunotherapy (AIT) achieves sustained drug-free remission in allergic asthma in ~30-40% of treated patients
Gap size
Moderate; highly severity-dependent; large practice gap for AIT
Primary barrier
biological (severe phenotype) + practice (AIT underutilized)
Allergen immunotherapy is the only disease-modifying treatment for allergic asthma and is underused relative to guidelines; biological phenotyping (T2-high vs T2-low) drives outcomes more than treatment intensity alone
