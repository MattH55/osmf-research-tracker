Research Tracker / Chronic Disease Interventions / Fibromyalgia

Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Partial spontaneous improvement in ~25-30% over 5 years; complete resolution without treatment rare; chronic fluctuating course in majority; central sensitisation often persists
Best-intervention remission
Multimodal approach (aerobic exercise + CBT + sleep optimisation): most effective combination — 50-60% significant improvement; duloxetine: 50% pain response in 43% vs 29% placebo; milnacipran: similar; pregabalin: 50% pain response 38-50%; low-dose naltrexone (LDN): 30% improvement in open-label studies
Gap size
Large — pharmacotherapy is modestly effective; exercise is the most effective single intervention but chronically underused as 'too painful'; disease is often dismissed
Primary barrier
framing/practice
Fibromyalgia was dismissed as non-organic by mainstream medicine for decades; this has resulted in: delayed diagnosis (mean 5 years), inappropriate investigations, ineffective treatments (opioids in particular), and under-referral to physiotherapy and psychological therapies; the 2010 ACR criteria removed tender point requirement and improved diagnosis
