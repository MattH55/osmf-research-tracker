Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Hashimoto's (most common cause) is irreversible in majority; spontaneous recovery in <5%; subclinical hypothyroidism spontaneously normalizes in ~30-40% of cases within 2 years without treatment
Best-intervention remission
T4 monotherapy normalizes TSH in nearly all patients and resolves symptoms in ~60%; up to 40% of T4-treated patients remain symptomatic despite normalized TSH; T4+T3 combination or desiccated thyroid extract (DTE) preferred by ~50% of patients in blinded preference trials
Gap size
Moderate — within the treated population, a large minority are inadequately treated by the standard-of-care T4 monotherapy approach
Primary barrier
framing/practice
Up to 40% of patients remain symptomatic on T4 despite guideline-defined adequate treatment; T3 combination and DTE are rarely offered in primary care despite guideline acknowledgment that a trial may be appropriate; TSH-only monitoring misses tissue-level hypothyroidism
