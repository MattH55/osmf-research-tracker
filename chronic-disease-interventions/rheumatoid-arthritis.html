Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
11% achieve drug-free remission (DFR) at median 74 months; most cohorts show 10-15% DFR ceiling
Best-intervention remission
Early aggressive treat-to-target improves DFR rates modestly; some biologic-era cohorts reach 20-25% DFR; on-drug remission rates (DAS28 <2.6) reach 40-60% with modern biologics and JAK inhibitors
Gap size
Small for drug-free remission (biological ceiling is genuinely low); large gap between on-drug remission and drug-free remission
Primary barrier
biological (DFR); practice (on-drug remission underachieved due to treatment inertia)
Treat-to-target strategy is guideline-recommended but poorly implemented in routine care; many patients remain on inadequate therapy without escalation
