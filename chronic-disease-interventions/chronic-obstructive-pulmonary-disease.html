Disease Overview

Spontaneous remission
Effectively zero — airflow obstruction is irreversible by definition in COPD; FEV1 decline continues at 30-60 mL/year on standard care
Best-intervention remission
Smoking cessation slows FEV1 decline to near-normal aging rate (the only intervention that does this); lung volume reduction surgery (LVRS) improves FEV1 15-20% in selected emphysema patients; 52% of COPD patients achieve clinically meaningful improvement after pulmonary rehabilitation
Gap size
Large for disease modification (smoking cessation the only real lever); moderate for functional outcomes (pulmonary rehab underused)
Primary barrier
biological + practice
Biological limit: structural lung damage is not reversible with any current pharmacotherapy. Practice gap: pulmonary rehabilitation achieves clinically meaningful improvement in 52% of patients but fewer than 2% of eligible COPD patients in the US receive it. 26% of patients with a COPD diagnosis have no evidence of airflow obstruction on spirometry (misdiagnosed, overtreated)
