Research Tracker / Chronic Disease Interventions / Parkinson's Disease

Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive neurodegeneration — no spontaneous remission; untreated motor decline averages 3-5 UPDRS points/year; non-motor symptoms (depression, cognitive decline, autonomic dysfunction) also progressive
Best-intervention remission
Levodopa/carbidopa: 40-50% motor improvement in early disease but efficacy wanes (motor fluctuations in 50% at 5 years, dyskinesias in 30%); deep brain stimulation (DBS): 50-60% reduction in off-time, 60-70% reduction in dyskinesias at 5 years; LCIG (levodopa-carbidopa intestinal gel infusion): near-continuous on-time in advanced patients
Gap size
Large — DBS significantly underused; no disease-modifying therapy exists; current treatments are purely symptomatic
Primary barrier
biological + practice
No neuroprotective or disease-modifying treatment is approved despite decades of research; DBS is offered too late in disease course (often 10+ years after diagnosis vs evidence for benefit in earlier disease — EARLYSTIM trial); focused ultrasound (FDA cleared 2021) underused as non-invasive alternative
