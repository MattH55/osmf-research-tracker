Disease Overview

Spontaneous remission
60-85% achieve seizure freedom on medication; of those, 50-75% remain seizure-free after withdrawal (varies by syndrome and duration of freedom)
Best-intervention remission
Not clearly higher than on-medication rates -- the real gap is behavioral/practice
Gap size
Small biological gap; large practice gap
Primary barrier
practice
Only 26-45% of seizure-free patients who are candidates for medication withdrawal ever attempt it; physician and patient reluctance vastly exceeds biological risk
