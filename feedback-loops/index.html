Feedback Loops That Maintain Low Energy in Long COVID / ME-CFS

Low energy in these conditions behaves like a stable attractor state, not a simple deficit — this page maps why it self-sustains: restorative loops held suppressed, vicious loops actively engaged. Every claim below carries a verified citation and an explicit evidence grade.

Heterogeneity caveat Long COVID and ME/CFS overlap but almost certainly contain subtypes with different dominant loops – for example a vascular/O₂-extraction-dominant phenotype versus an autoimmune/autonomic-dominant one. The unified diagram below over-fits a heterogeneous population by construction. Treat it as a map of candidate mechanisms that may each apply to a subset of patients, not a single pathway that explains every case.
Why not just "less ATP"? The naive model – fatigue as a simple energy deficit – is the frame this page complicates. Bulk cellular [ATP] is tightly homeostatically defended, and a substantial share of clinical fatigue is understood as a regulated central-nervous-system output (an effort-cost computation), not a direct read-out of substrate depletion. The loops below describe the upstream biology that could drive that regulated output, and the suppressed repair pathways that would normally resolve it.
