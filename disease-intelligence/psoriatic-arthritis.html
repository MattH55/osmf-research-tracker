Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous remission uncommon (<5%); progressive joint damage in 40-60% without adequate treatment; enthesitis, dactylitis, axial involvement accumulate over time
Best-intervention remission
IL-17 inhibitors: ACR50 response 58-62%, MDA (minimal disease activity) in 40-50%; IL-23 inhibitors (guselkumab, risankizumab): similar efficacy with better skin clearance; JAK inhibitors (upadacitinib): ACR50 71% at 26 weeks; drug-free remission in ~15% in early PsA with T2T
Drug-free remission
—
Relapse after remission
—
Chronicity
—
Gap size
Large — most patients are undertreated on methotrexate or NSAIDs alone; treat-to-target poorly implemented
Primary barrier
practice/access
Barrier detail: Diagnostic delay (mean 5-7 years from symptom onset to PsA diagnosis); methotrexate has modest efficacy in PsA but is gatekept as mandatory step before biologic access in many health systems; treat-to-target (MDA as goal) poorly implemented; most patients cycle through multiple NSAIDs before biologic access

Last SoC change: 2023 — upadacitinib first-line biologic approved for PsA; deucravacitinib (TYK2) Phase 3 data; 2020 guselkumab/risankizumab for PsA; tofacitinib/ixekizumab since 2017-2018; anti-TNF standard since 2003
