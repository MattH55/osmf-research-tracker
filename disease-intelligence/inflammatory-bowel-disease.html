Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Disease course unchanged in ~85% of patients at 10 years on standard care; ~23% (CD) and ~12% (UC) relapse within 12 months of thiopurine withdrawal in sustained remission
Best-intervention remission
Anti-IL-23 biologics (mirikizumab, risankizumab) maintain steroid-free remission in >60% at 2 years — but on the drug, not drug-free

Right to Try Relevance

Eligibility: This disease meets the federal and state right-to-try legal standard (21 U.S.C. § 360bbb-0a and Montana SB 535): it lacks disease-modifying therapy with indication-specific FDA approval. Patients with Inflammatory Bowel Disease may be eligible to access investigational therapies outside of clinical trials under right-to-try frameworks.
