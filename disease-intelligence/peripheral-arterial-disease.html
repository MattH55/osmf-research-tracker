Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive disease; spontaneous improvement rare; limb ischaemia worsens in 25-30% over 5 years; major adverse limb events (MALE) in 10-15%/year in advanced PAD; major amputation in 5-10% at 5 years without revascularisation
Best-intervention remission
Supervised exercise therapy (SET): equivalent to endovascular revascularisation for claudication at 6-12 months (CLEVER trial); revascularisation: 70-80% 1-year limb salvage; SGLT2 inhibitors + GLP-1 agonists reduce MALE by 15-20% (AMPLITUDE-O, EMPA-REG); rivaroxaban + aspirin (COMPASS trial): 46% reduction in MALE

Gap size
Very large — supervised exercise therapy is first-line in guidelines but almost no patient is referred; PAD remains vastly underdiagnosed
Primary barrier
access/framing
Barrier detail: PAD is underdiagnosed (ABI testing rarely performed in primary care); supervised exercise therapy is guideline Class IA recommendation but reimbursed and accessible for <5% of PAD patients; cilostazol (PDE3 inhibitor) improves walking but is contraindicated in heart failure (common comorbidity); PAD mortality often exceeds coronary disease mortality in this population

Last SoC change: 2024 — rivaroxaban + aspirin (COMPASS) now guideline-recommended; 2023 SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 agonists recognised for limb protection; 2022 VOYAGER-PAD (rivaroxaban post-revascularisation); supervised exercise therapy Class IA since 2016; cilostazol approved 1999
