Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
No spontaneous remission; progressive organ damage; median survival historically 40-60 years but improving; vaso-occlusive crises (VOC) in 1-4 per year average
Best-intervention remission
Haematopoietic stem cell transplant (HSCT): curative in 90-95% if matched sibling donor available; hydroxyurea reduces VOC frequency 50%, ACS risk 50%; gene therapy (exagamglogene autotemcel [exa-cel]/Casgevy): 30/31 patients free from severe VOC through 24 months; betibeglogene (Zynteglo): 90% reduction in severe VOC
Drug-free remission
—
Relapse after remission
—
Chronicity
—
Gap size
Very large — HSCT is curative but only 20% have matched donors; gene therapy is curative but costs $2-3M; hydroxyurea is dramatically underprescribed
Primary barrier
access
Barrier detail: Hydroxyurea is inexpensive, highly effective, and generic but prescribed to only 25-35% of eligible SCD patients (racial and healthcare access disparities prominent); gene therapies (Casgevy, Zynteglo) approved December 2023 represent first curative options without matched donor — but $2-3M price tag makes access essentially impossible without negotiation

Last SoC change: December 2023 — FDA approved exagamglogene autotemcel (Casgevy, CRISPR-based) and betibeglogene (Zynteglo) — first CRISPR-based gene therapy approvals anywhere in medicine; voxelotor (2019); crizanlizumab (2019); hydroxyurea standard since 1998
