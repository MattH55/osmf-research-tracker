Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive natural history; symptoms worsen in 50-60% over 5 years without treatment; spontaneous improvement in 20-30% (particularly mild symptoms); acute urinary retention risk ~1-2%/year
Best-intervention remission
Combination alpha-blocker + 5-ARI (tamsulosin + dutasteride): reduces prostate volume 25-30%, acute urinary retention risk 67% (CombAT trial); minimally invasive procedures (Rezum, Urolift, Aquablation): IPSS improvement 50-60%, preserving ejaculatory function better than TURP; TURP: gold standard, 70-80% symptom improvement

—

Gap size
Moderate — treatment is effective but procedure uptake and pharmacotherapy titration is suboptimal; many patients overtreated with TURP when less invasive options appropriate
Primary barrier
practice
Barrier detail: Alpha-blockers provide symptomatic relief but do not address prostate volume in large glands (5-ARI needed); TURP remains widely used when Rezum/Urolift preserve sexual function with similar efficacy; pharmacotherapy stepped-up without referral in cases where early MIS procedure would preserve more function

Last SoC change: 2023 — Aquablation FDA approved; 2019 Rezum (water vapour therapy) established; 2016 Urolift established; 5-ARI added to alpha-blocker standard since CombAT trial 2008; alpha-blockers first-line since 1990s; TURP gold standard since 1960s
