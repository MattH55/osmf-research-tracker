Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous passage without intervention: 70-80% of stones <4 mm; 40-50% of stones 4-6mm; <25% of stones >6mm; recurrence in 50% within 10 years without metabolic assessment and prevention
Best-intervention remission
Alpha-1 blockers (tamsulosin, 0.4mg): facilitate passage 30-45% faster for distal ureteral stones; ESWL: 75-80% stone-free for <2cm renal stones; PCNL: 90%+ stone-free for >2cm stones; dietary and metabolic prevention: 50% recurrence reduction with hydration + diet (STOP-JNKi-like approach)

Gap size
Moderate — metabolic evaluation and dietary prevention are highly effective but chronically underperformed after first stone
Primary barrier
practice
Barrier detail: Only 10-20% of first-time kidney stone patients receive a 24-hour urine metabolic evaluation that would guide specific dietary and pharmacological prevention; high fluid intake (>2.5L/day) is evidence-based and free but rarely systematically emphasised; recurrence is highly preventable with appropriate dietary modification

Last SoC change: 2019 — AUA/EAU guidelines updated on 24-hour urine evaluation (recommend for all recurrent stone formers, single stone in high-risk); ESWL vs URS evidence updated; no pharmacological paradigm change; thiazide diuretics and citrate supplementation standard since 1980s
