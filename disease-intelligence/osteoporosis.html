Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Bone density decline of 1-3%/year in postmenopausal women without treatment; fracture risk increases exponentially with T-score decline; no spontaneous remission
Best-intervention remission
Bisphosphonates: 40-50% reduction in vertebral fractures, 20-40% hip fracture reduction; romosozumab (anti-sclerostin): 73% vertebral fracture reduction at 12 months (ARCH trial); abaloparatide/teriparatide (anabolic): BMD increase 9-12% at lumbar spine over 18 months

Gap size
Very large — post-fracture care gap is stark: only 20-30% of patients with fragility fracture receive osteoporosis treatment within 1 year
Primary barrier
practice/framing
Barrier detail: The 'post-fracture care gap' is one of the most documented practice failures in medicine; the majority of fragility fracture patients leave hospital without osteoporosis diagnosis or treatment; fracture liaison services (FLS) fix this but are not universally implemented; anabolic therapies (romosozumab, teriparatide) drastically underused vs anti-resorptives

Last SoC change: 2023 — NOF/ASBMR guidelines recommend anabolic therapy first-line in very high-risk patients (T-score <-3.0 or prior hip/vertebral fracture); romosozumab approved 2019; 2016 GIOP guidelines updated; bisphosphonates standard since mid-1990s
