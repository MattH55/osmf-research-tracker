Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Venous leg ulcers: spontaneous healing in 20-30% at 6 months without compression; recurrence in 70% within 1 year even after healing; CVI is progressive without intervention
Best-intervention remission
Compression therapy (graduated 40 mmHg): 70-80% healing at 24 weeks; venous ablation (EVLA, RFA) of incompetent veins + compression: 57% ulcer healing at 6 months vs 36% compression alone (VenUS IV); early intervention (within 6 months of ulcer onset) dramatically improves outcomes

Gap size
Large — venous ablation is significantly underused; compression adherence is the primary barrier in healed ulcers
Primary barrier
practice/access
Barrier detail: The Early Venous Reflux Ablation (EVRA) trial showed early ablation superior to compression-first strategy but most patients still wait 6+ months; compression stocking adherence is extremely poor without adequate education; many patients receive only wound dressings without compression or vascular assessment

Last SoC change: 2018 — EVRA trial published (early ablation as first-line for VLU with reflux); European guidelines 2015; compression as mainstay unchanged for decades; foam sclerotherapy/EVLA since early 2000s
