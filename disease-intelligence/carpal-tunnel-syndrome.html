Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Mild CTS: spontaneous resolution in 30-40% within 12 months without treatment; moderate-severe: <5% spontaneous resolution; pregnancy-related CTS resolves in 80% post-delivery
Best-intervention remission
Surgical decompression (carpal tunnel release, CTR): 90% symptom resolution at 12 months; 85% satisfaction at 5 years; superior to splinting at 6 months (SEVIORAL trial); corticosteroid injection: 75% improvement at 4 weeks, 50% at 6 months, 20% at 12 months

Gap size
Small — surgery is highly effective; the question is appropriate timing and selecting patients for non-surgical management
Primary barrier
practice/framing
Barrier detail: Surgery is underused in some health systems where patients are kept on conservative management (splinting, cortisone) for years with progressive nerve damage; conversely, overused in mild cases where non-surgical management could suffice; ergonomic intervention is chronically under-addressed

Last SoC change: 2016 — AAOS CTS guideline updated; surgical release remains gold standard since 1970s; endoscopic vs open technique evidence established 2000s; no paradigm change
