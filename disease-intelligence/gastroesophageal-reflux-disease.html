Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Symptom recurrence in 70-80% within 6 months of PPI discontinuation in erosive GERD; highly variable in non-erosive disease; ~20% of GERD is nonerosive, where acid suppression is less effective
Best-intervention remission
Weight loss of >10% reduces symptoms in 65-81% of obese patients; laparoscopic anti-reflux surgery (Nissen fundoplication) achieves long-term symptom control in 85-92% and eliminates daily PPI need in majority; bariatric surgery in obese patients near-eliminates GERD

Primary barrier
framing/practice
Barrier detail: AGA 2022 guidelines explicitly recommend PPI de-escalation or discontinuation in patients without erosive esophagitis or Barrett's — yet 84 million monthly Medicare PPI claims in 2019 suggest massive overprescription; most patients are never offered surgical or intensive lifestyle options

Last SoC change: 2022 — AGA Clinical Practice Update on PPI de-prescribing (explicit guidance to attempt discontinuation in low-risk patients); 2022 vonoprazan (P-CAB) approved as alternative; PPI-first strategy unchanged since 1989
