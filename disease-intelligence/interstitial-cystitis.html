Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Partial spontaneous remission in 20-30% at 2 years; complete resolution rare; chronic pain condition with significant disability; no biomarker-defined diagnosis complicates studies
Best-intervention remission
Multimodal approach (dietary modification + physical therapy + neuromodulation + intravesical therapy): 50-70% overall treatment response but rarely complete remission; intravesical heparin + lidocaine + sodium bicarbonate: 70-80% short-term response; sacral neuromodulation: 48-60% significant improvement at 2 years; cyclosporine A (oral): 75% improvement in Hunner lesion IC vs 19% placebo

Gap size
Very large — no FDA-approved treatment; management is symptomatic and highly individualised
Primary barrier
biological + practice
Barrier detail: Only pentosan polysulfate sodium (Elmiron, FDA approved 1996) has FDA approval for IC/BPS, and this is now associated with maculopathy (pigmentary maculopathy) with long-term use; Hunner lesion IC (25% of cases) is a distinct pathological entity with different treatment response than non-Hunner IC; diagnostic heterogeneity makes clinical trials extremely difficult

Last SoC change: 2022 — AUA IC/BPS guideline updated; Elmiron maculopathy warning strengthened; no new pharmacotherapy approval; pentosan polysulfate sodium only approved drug since 1996
