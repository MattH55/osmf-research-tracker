Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Essentially zero for established T1DM (honeymoon phase occurs in 50-60% after diagnosis but lasts 3-12 months then ends); the disease is permanent autoimmune destruction of beta cells
Best-intervention remission
Closed-loop insulin delivery (artificial pancreas systems — Control-IQ, Omnipod 5) achieves 70-80% time-in-range vs 60% with standard therapy; teplizumab (anti-CD3 mAb) delays T1DM onset by 2+ years in at-risk individuals; islet cell transplantation achieves insulin independence in ~44% at 5 years (CITR data)

Gap size
Moderate-large — closed-loop approaches are increasingly standard but true insulin independence is rare with any available therapy
Primary barrier
biological + access
Barrier detail: Beta cell regeneration and transplantation face immune rejection barriers (all islet recipients require chronic immunosuppression); closed-loop systems dramatically improve quality of life and glycemic control but require intensive engagement and are expensive; teplizumab (FDA approved 2022) for delay is not a cure

Last SoC change: 2022 — teplizumab (Tzield) FDA approved for Stage 2 T1DM (first immunotherapy to delay progression); 2023 closed-loop systems widely recommended; 2021 Omnipod 5 cleared; ADA continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) recommended for all T1DM 2020; insulin unchanged as backbone since 1921
