Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous drug-free remission: 2-10% in some cohorts over 5 years; disease-free survival in 15-20% at 20 years; renal lupus (LN) progression to ESRD in 10-20% within 20 years on standard care
Best-intervention remission
Triple therapy (hydroxychloroquine + MMF + belimumab): 43% complete renal response at 52 weeks (BLISS-LN); voclosporin + MMF: 40.8% complete renal response; anifrolumab (anti-IFNAR): 47.8% BICLA response in active SLE (TULIP-2); aggressive treat-to-target achieves low disease activity in 60-70%

Primary barrier
access/practice
Barrier detail: Hydroxychloroquine (the safest, most evidence-based lupus drug) was underprescribed even before COVID-19 controversy; biologics (belimumab, anifrolumab) approved since 2011 and 2021 respectively but used in <15% of eligible patients; racial disparities profound (Black/Hispanic patients have 2-3x higher disease severity and mortality but lower biologic access)

Last SoC change: 2021 — anifrolumab (Saphnelo, anti-IFN-type I) FDA approved — first new mechanism in decade; voclosporin (Lupkynis) for LN approved 2021; belimumab since 2011; hydroxychloroquine as backbone since 1950s; mycophenolate since 2000
