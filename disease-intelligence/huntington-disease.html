Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Inevitable progressive neurodegeneration in all carriers of HTT CAG expansion >36 repeats; no spontaneous remission; motor, cognitive, psychiatric decline universally progressive
Best-intervention remission
Tetrabenazine/deutetrabenazine (VMAT2 inhibitors): 25-30% reduction in chorea score (FIRST-HD); no disease-modifying treatment proven; genetic CSF biomarker (mutant huntingtin, mHTT) reduction achieved with tominersen ASO — but Phase 3 halted 2021 (higher dose harmful); lower-dose tominersen now in Phase 2/3 after exploratory biomarker signal

Gap size
Very large — no approved disease-modifying treatment; symptomatic treatment only
Primary barrier
biological
Barrier detail: HD is unique in having a perfectly predictive genetic test allowing pre-symptomatic intervention — the challenge is that trials of mHTT-lowering agents (tominersen, WVE-003) have faced setbacks; the therapeutic window (pre-manifest vs manifest disease) and appropriate dose remain unclear; genetic counselling for presymptomatic testing is complex

Last SoC change: 2023 — valbenazine (Ingrezza) exploring HD chorea; 2021 tominersen Phase 3 halted (unexpectedly worse in higher-dose arm); 2017 deutetrabenazine (Austedo) approved for HD chorea; 2008 tetrabenazine approved; no disease-modifying treatment since disease characterised

Right to Try Relevance Critical

Eligibility: This disease meets the federal and state right-to-try legal standard (21 U.S.C. § 360bbb-0a and Montana SB 535): it lacks disease-modifying therapy with indication-specific FDA approval. Patients with Huntington Disease may be eligible to access investigational therapies outside of clinical trials under right-to-try frameworks.
