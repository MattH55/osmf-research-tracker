Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive optic nerve damage without treatment; average visual field loss 0.5-1.0 dB/year; blindness in 10-20% without treatment; no spontaneous recovery of lost vision
Best-intervention remission
IOP-lowering drops: 20-30% IOP reduction; OHTS trial showed 50% risk reduction for glaucoma development in ocular hypertension; SLT (selective laser trabeculoplasty): IOP reduction 25-30%, equivalent to drops at 3 years (LiGHT trial); trabeculectomy: 35-45% IOP reduction, superior to drops in advanced disease

Gap size
Moderate — IOP control is achievable but compliance is the dominant barrier; neuroprotection remains an unmet need
Primary barrier
biological (neuroprotection) + practice (adherence)
Barrier detail: IOP-lowering prevents further damage but cannot restore lost vision; adherence to drop therapy is poor (50-70% of patients non-adherent at 1 year); SLT (laser) as first-line (recommended by LiGHT trial) not universally adopted; once-yearly drop (latanoprostene bunod) and sustained-release implants (Durysta) improving adherence

Last SoC change: 2023 — bimatoprost SR implant (Durysta) expanding to real-world use; 2021 Santen DE-117 sustained-release technology; 2019 rho kinase inhibitor (netarsudil/Rhopressa) approved — new mechanism; prostaglandin analogs as first-line since late 1990s; LiGHT trial 2019 established SLT as superior first-line to drops
