Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Structural progression is essentially universal over 10+ years without intervention; pain fluctuates but functional decline is the expected trajectory; no disease-modifying therapy exists as of 2024
Best-intervention remission
No cure or disease modification available; total joint replacement (TJR) achieves 85-90% patient satisfaction and near-elimination of pain but is not disease reversal; weight loss of >10% reduces pain scores ~50% and slows structural progression; exercise therapy comparable to NSAIDs for pain relief

Primary barrier
biological + practice
Barrier detail: OARSI formally petitioned the FDA in 2016 to recognize OA as a serious disease with no cure and no disease-modifying interventions — confirmed in FDA's criteria. Practice gap: NSAIDs have been first-line for >100 years despite long-term evidence of joint damage acceleration and GI/CV risk; exercise consistently outperforms medication in functional outcomes but is rarely 'prescribed'

Last SoC change: 2019-2020 — OARSI/ACR guidelines formally de-emphasized acetaminophen (evidence of minimal efficacy) and added conditional recommendation against opioids; NSAIDs have been first-line since the 1960s; duloxetine added ~2010 as centrally-acting option
