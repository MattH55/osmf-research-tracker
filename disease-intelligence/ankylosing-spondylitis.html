Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive structural damage (sacroiliac fusion, syndesmophytes) in majority without treatment; spontaneous radiographic remission essentially zero; ~10% achieve clinical remission without anti-inflammatory therapy
Best-intervention remission
TNF inhibitors (infliximab, adalimumab): ASAS20 response in 60-70%, clinical remission (ASDAS-ID) in 20-40%; IL-17 inhibitors (secukinumab, ixekizumab): equivalent or superior efficacy; remission rates with early biologics in non-radiographic axSpA may reach 40-50%

Primary barrier
practice/access
Barrier detail: Diagnostic delay of 7-10 years from symptom onset is the most significant modifiable factor; MRI-based diagnosis of nr-axSpA (non-radiographic) can enable earlier biologic initiation before structural damage; HLA-B27 testing remains underused in primary care assessment of inflammatory back pain

Last SoC change: 2023 — bimekizumab (dual IL-17A/F) approved for AS; 2019 secukinumab and ixekizumab established for AS/nr-axSpA; tofacitinib and upadacitinib (JAK inhibitors) added; TNF inhibitors as backbone since 2000
