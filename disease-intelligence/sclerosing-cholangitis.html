Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive cholestatic disease; median time to liver failure or transplant 12-15 years; spontaneous remission essentially zero; biliary strictures progress in majority; cholangiocarcinoma risk 10-15% lifetime
Best-intervention remission
Liver transplantation: 5-year survival 80-85% (curative but recurrence in 20% post-transplant); ursodeoxycholic acid (UDCA): improvement in LFTs but no transplant-free survival benefit (Phase 3 — no benefit, possibly harm at high dose); no pharmacotherapy proven to alter the natural history

Gap size
Very large — no approved disease-modifying therapy exists; liver transplantation is the only definitive treatment
Primary barrier
biological
Barrier detail: PSC has resisted all pharmacotherapy attempts; UDCA widely prescribed despite no survival benefit (at high doses — possibly harmful); the field has 20+ failed Phase 2/3 trials; norUDCA, nor-UDCA, and several FXR agonists are in Phase 3; gut-liver axis and gut microbiome increasingly implicated (80% of PSC patients have concurrent IBD)

Last SoC change: 2023 — multiple Phase 3 trials underway but no approval; UDCA re-evaluated (Phase 3 Mayo trial showed no benefit); annual colon and biliary cancer surveillance as major guideline update; no approved pharmacotherapy to date
