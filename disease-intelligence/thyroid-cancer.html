Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Not applicable — surgical resection is standard; papillary microcarcinoma (<1 cm, low-risk): active surveillance achieves 99.8%+ cancer-specific survival at 10 years without immediate surgery in Japanese cohorts
Best-intervention remission
Total thyroidectomy + RAI for intermediate/high-risk: 90-95% disease-free survival at 10 years; active surveillance for low-risk papillary microcarcinoma: non-inferior to surgery in multiple cohorts; lenvatinib + pembrolizumab for RAI-refractory: OS benefit (LEAP-011 ongoing); vemurafenib for BRAF V600E mutant: response in 38%
Drug-free remission
—
Relapse after remission
—
Chronicity
—
Gap size
Moderate — overtreatment of low-risk disease is the dominant problem (too many total thyroidectomies in low-risk disease)
Primary barrier
practice/framing
Barrier detail: The Kuma Hospital and Cancer Institute Hospital experience in Japan showing non-inferiority of active surveillance for low-risk papillary microcarcinoma has not been widely adopted outside Japan; total thyroidectomy with radioiodine for all differentiated thyroid cancer remains common despite lack of benefit in low-risk disease

Last SoC change: 2023 — ATA task force expanded active surveillance indications; lenvatinib now standard second-line; 2018 ATA guidelines reduced RAI use in low-risk; total thyroidectomy + RAI standard since 1990s (largely unchanged)
