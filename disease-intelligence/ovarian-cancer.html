Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
No spontaneous remission; 5-year survival Stage I: 90%; Stage III/IV: 25-30%; most patients (75%) present at Stage III-IV
Best-intervention remission
Debulking surgery + carboplatin/paclitaxel: 70-80% initial response; PARP inhibitors (olaparib, rucaparib) maintenance in BRCA-mutant: 56% 5-year PFS vs 13.8% placebo (SOLO-1); bevacizumab maintenance: modest PFS benefit; dose-dense paclitaxel: 72.1% vs 65.1% OS at 3 years

Gap size
Large — PARP inhibitors dramatically improve outcomes in BRCA-mutant patients; HRD testing not universal; no effective screening exists
Primary barrier
access + biological
Barrier detail: BRCA1/2 and HRD testing is not universal despite major impact on PARP inhibitor eligibility; bevacizumab access limited by cost; most patients are diagnosed at advanced stage due to no screening modality; CA-125 + transvaginal ultrasound screening trials (UKCTOCS) failed to show mortality reduction

Last SoC change: 2023 — mirvetuximab soravtansine (IMGN853, FRα-ADC) approved for FRα-high platinum-resistant OC; 2022 olaparib maintenance expanded; 2018 PARP inhibitor maintenance established; carboplatin/paclitaxel standard since 1990s
