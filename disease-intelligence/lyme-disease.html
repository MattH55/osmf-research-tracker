Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Acute Lyme: spontaneous resolution in some untreated patients but dissemination and late manifestations develop; PTLDS (persistent symptoms after adequate antibiotics): affects 10-20% of patients; spontaneous resolution of PTLDS in 50-60% by 6 months
Best-intervention remission
Acute Lyme: 14-21 day doxycycline achieves 80-95% cure; PTLDS: no treatment shown superior to placebo in 4 large RCTs; prolonged antibiotic therapy (IDSA-condemned) not effective; symptom management only

Gap size
Large for PTLDS — the field lacks an evidence-based treatment and has been mired in controversy over mechanism and management
Primary barrier
biological (PTLDS mechanism uncertain) + framing (controversy)
Barrier detail: PTLDS treatment is one of the most contentious areas in medicine; IDSA position (no benefit of prolonged antibiotics) vs ILADS position (chronic infection requiring extended treatment) — RCTs support IDSA; the patient experience of persistent symptoms is real but the mechanism may not be ongoing active infection; this creates a significant care gap for suffering patients

Last SoC change: 2020 — IDSA/AAN/ACR joint guidelines updated (no significant change in management; prolonged antibiotics explicitly not recommended); ongoing NIH investment in PTLDS mechanism research post-COVID-19 analogy with Long COVID
