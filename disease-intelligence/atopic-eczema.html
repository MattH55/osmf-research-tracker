Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
60-70% of childhood-onset AD achieves spontaneous remission by adolescence; adult-onset AD is more persistent; moderate-severe adult AD: <15% spontaneous clearance at 5 years
Best-intervention remission
Dupilumab (anti-IL-4Rα): 51% IGA 0/1 (clear/almost clear) at 16 weeks in SOLO trials; tralokinumab (anti-IL-13): 38% clear/almost clear; baricitinib (JAK1/2i): 16% EASI-100 (complete clearance) at 16 weeks; upadacitinib (JAK1i): 28-40% EASI-100; near-complete clearance now achievable for most patients

Gap size
Large — most patients managed with topical steroids for decades without biologic assessment; biologics available since 2017 but reach only a fraction of eligible patients
Primary barrier
access/practice
Barrier detail: Dupilumab approved 2017 but still not first-line in most health systems due to cost ($36,000/year); step therapy requirements force patients through years of topical steroids and immunosuppressants first; racial disparities in specialist access and biologic prescribing

Last SoC change: 2022-2023 — JAK inhibitors (upadacitinib, baricitinib) and tralokinumab added; lebrikizumab approved 2023; dupilumab since 2017; prior standard (topical steroids, tacrolimus, cyclosporine) essentially unchanged since 1980s-2000s
