Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous remission in ~10-15% over 2 years without treatment; IBM (inclusion body myositis) is slowly progressive with no effective treatment; death from interstitial lung disease in dermatomyositis in 5-10%
Best-intervention remission
High-dose corticosteroids + IVIG + steroid-sparing agent (methotrexate/azathioprine): clinical remission in 40-60% of DM/PM; IVIG (ProQolity, FDA approved 2021 for DM): 79% response vs 44% placebo (PROPACK trial); rituximab in refractory: 77% response (RIM trial)

Gap size
Large for IBM (no effective treatment); moderate for DM/PM
Primary barrier
biological (IBM) + practice (DM/PM undertreated)
Barrier detail: IBM (the most common myopathy in those >60 years) has no effective treatment despite multiple trial failures (bimagrumab, arimoclomol); DM/PM are managed with high-dose steroids first despite substantial side effects and emerging evidence for biologic first-line approaches

Last SoC change: 2021 — IVIG approved specifically for dermatomyositis (PROPACK Phase 3 — first RCT ever positive for any DM treatment); rituximab widely used off-label; corticosteroids as first-line since 1960s; EULAR/ACR classification criteria updated 2017
