Monogenic Diseases & Gene Therapy Approaches

This mapper identifies diseases caused by single-gene mutations and their corresponding gene therapy strategies:

  • Gene Replacement: Deliver functional copies via viral vectors (AAV, lentiviral, adenoviral) to restore protein function
  • RNA Interference: Silence disease-causing alleles using antisense oligonucleotides or siRNA (e.g., nusinersen for SMA)
  • CRISPR/Base Editing: Correct mutations in-situ using genome editing (e.g., exagamglogene autotemcel for RPE65-LCA)
  • Protein Replacement: Enzyme replacement therapy (ERT) for lysosomal storage diseases

Clinical trials count reflects active and completed studies from ClinicalTrials.gov.
