Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive with worsening glycaemic control; reversal essentially not possible with current treatments once established; spontaneous improvement only in cases of rapid glycaemic normalisation early in course
Best-intervention remission
Intensive glycaemic control in T1DM (DCCT): 60% reduction in neuropathy development; pain management (duloxetine, pregabalin): 30-50% pain reduction but no disease modification; alpha-lipoic acid IV: nerve conduction velocity improvement at 3 weeks (SYDNEY 2 trial); SGLT2 inhibitors: emerging data on neuropathy protection

Gap size
Very large — no disease-modifying therapy approved; pain treatment is the only intervention most patients receive
Primary barrier
biological (pain modification) + practice (prevention gap)
Barrier detail: Prevention through glycaemic control is highly effective but prevention means tight glycaemic control for decades; pain treatment gives symptomatic relief only; opioid prescribing for DPN pain remains common despite guidelines against it; few patients are offered alpha-lipoic acid which has the strongest disease-modification signal

Last SoC change: 2023 — FDA approved TENS device (Quell 2.0) for DPN pain; 2022 DPNP guidance from ADA updated; duloxetine and pregabalin as first-line since 2004-2005; no paradigm change for 20 years
