Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Chronic and progressive; glandular dysfunction (dry eyes/mouth) irreversible in most; fatigue and systemic features fluctuate; lymphoma risk 5-15x general population; spontaneous full remission rare
Best-intervention remission
Hydroxychloroquine: limited systemic benefit; rituximab: parotid gland improvement and fatigue in some trials but primary endpoint failure in Phase 3 (TEARS, TRACTISS); no disease-modifying therapy approved; best outcomes with early systematic sicca management

Gap size

Primary barrier
biological + access
Barrier detail: Sjögren's research has been hampered by heterogeneous disease and lack of validated endpoints; rituximab failed multiple Phase 3 trials despite positive Phase 2 data (endpoint selection problem); anti-Ro/SSA positivity enriches trials but treatment effects still modest; diagnosis itself is delayed 4-7 years on average

Last SoC change: 2023 — iscalimab (anti-CD40) Phase 3 data; no disease-modifying therapy approved; EULAR recommendations updated 2020; hydroxychloroquine as standard systemic agent since 1990s
