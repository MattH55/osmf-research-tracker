Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Early-stage (Rai 0-I): watch-and-wait appropriate in 50-70%; spontaneous remission extremely rare; median time to treatment in Rai Stage 0: 10+ years; Richter's transformation (5-10%) extremely poor prognosis
Best-intervention remission
Venetoclax + obinutuzumab (CLL14): 2-year fixed duration, MRD-undetectable remission in 36%, PFS benefit sustained at 5 years vs chlorambucil; ibrutinib continuous: PFS benefit but not curative; BRUIN CLL trial (pirtobrutinib): 73% ORR in ibrutinib-resistant CLL
Drug-free remission
—
Relapse after remission

Gap size
Large — chlorambucil still used in some elderly patients; BTK inhibitor choice and venetoclax combination timing are practice-variable
Primary barrier
practice/access
Barrier detail: Chemoimmunotherapy (FCR, BR) largely replaced by BTK inhibitors and venetoclax but chlorambucil still used where cost is a barrier; the 'best drug at first relapse' question is evolving rapidly; CAR-T not yet standard for CLL

Last SoC change: 2023 — pirtobrutinib (non-covalent BTKi) for ibrutinib-resistant CLL; 2020 venetoclax + obinutuzumab fixed-duration; 2019 ibrutinib first-line approved; fludarabine + cyclophosphamide + rituximab (FCR) as standard 2006-2019; chlorambucil since 1950s
