Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Full recovery without intervention: <5% of adult ME/CFS patients; partial improvement in ~40% over 5 years; post-COVID ME/CFS appears to have similarly low spontaneous recovery rate
Best-intervention remission
No disease-modifying treatment established; GET (graded exercise therapy) — former standard — shown harmful (PACE controversy, withdrawn from UK NICE guidance 2021); pacing (heart rate-based) prevents post-exertional malaise but not curative; low-dose naltrexone — ~30% improvement in small RCTs; rintatolimod (Ampligen) — Phase 3 failed FDA approval

Primary barrier
biological (no treatment) + framing (historic)
Barrier detail: ME/CFS research has been systematically underfunded relative to disease burden (NIH funding $15M/year vs MS at $100M despite similar prevalence); PACE trial's GET/CBT approach adopted globally for years despite being harmful for many patients; NIH RECOVER initiative now represents largest-ever investment

Last SoC change: 2021 — NICE guidelines withdrew GET and CBT as primary treatments (major paradigm shift); 2024 NIH RECOVER Long COVID ME/CFS protocols underway; no approved pharmacotherapy exists; pacing as only widely recommended intervention
