Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Migraine frequency fluctuates; spontaneous remission (no attacks for 12+ months) occurs in ~25% over 10-year follow-up; 40% of migraine sufferers qualify for preventive therapy but only 3-13% receive it
Best-intervention remission
CGRP monoclonal antibodies (erenumab, fremanezumab, galcanezumab) achieve ≥50% reduction in monthly migraine days in 50-70% of patients; complete response (near-zero days) in 10-30%; acute triptans achieve 2-hour pain freedom in 30-40%

Primary barrier
practice/access
Barrier detail: Only 3-13% of the ~10 million US adults who qualify for preventive therapy receive it; prior authorization barriers for CGRP mAbs are substantial; widespread underdiagnosis; opioid prescribing for migraine in ED associated with medication overuse headache

Last SoC change: 2018-2020 — FDA approved first CGRP-targeted preventive therapies (erenumab 2018, fremanezumab/galcanezumab 2018-2019, atogepant 2021); prior prevention (beta-blockers, topiramate, amitriptyline) unchanged since 1990s-2000s; triptans first-line acute since 1993
