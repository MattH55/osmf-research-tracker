Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Childhood ITP: spontaneous remission in 70-80% within 6 months; Adult ITP: spontaneous remission in 20-30% at 1 year without treatment; chronic ITP (>12 months) rarely resolves spontaneously
Best-intervention remission
Rituximab: 40-60% initial response, 20-25% durable at 5 years; thrombopoietin receptor agonists (TPO-RA: eltrombopag, romiplostim): 70-90% response rate; splenectomy: 60-70% long-term complete response; fostamatinib: 18% CR in refractory; avatrombopag and lusutrombopag in perioperative settings

Gap size
Moderate — TPO-RAs are highly effective but chronic use required in most; splenectomy (durable cure in 65%) is underused in appropriate patients
Primary barrier
practice
Barrier detail: Splenectomy offers durable cure in 65% of ITP patients but is increasingly deferred in favour of chronic TPO-RA therapy which requires lifelong administration; the optimal sequence (corticosteroids → rituximab → TPO-RA → splenectomy) is debated; overtreated mild cases (platelet >30K without bleeding) remain a clinical challenge

Last SoC change: 2023 — rilzabrutinib (BTK inhibitor) Phase 3 LUNA data; daprodustat + avatrombopag combinations; 2021 fostamatinib (SYK inhibitor) added; 2008 romiplostim/eltrombopag first-line TPO-RAs; splenectomy standard since early 20th century
