Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
NMIBC: 50-70% recurrence within 5 years; MIBC: progression to metastatic in 50% without radical treatment; spontaneous remission essentially zero
Best-intervention remission
NMIBC: BCG intravesical immunotherapy: 70% reduction in recurrence, 30-40% reduction in progression; MIBC with neoadjuvant cisplatin + radical cystectomy: 5-year survival 50-60% (vs 45% cystectomy alone); metastatic with enfortumab vedotin + pembrolizumab: 31.5 months OS vs 16.1 months cisplatin (EV-302/KEYNOTE-A39)

Gap size
Large — EV + pembrolizumab is a major advance but not yet universally standard; BCG supply shortage is a persistent clinical crisis in NMIBC
Primary barrier
access
Barrier detail: BCG shortage (since 2012, persistent) means many NMIBC patients cannot receive standard treatment; gemcitabine + mitomycin as alternative to BCG established as FDA-cleared 2024 (CISTO trial); EV + pembrolizumab is a genuine paradigm change for metastatic disease but requires PD-L1 testing integration

Last SoC change: 2023 — EV + pembrolizumab (EV-302) established as first-line for cisplatin-eligible metastatic UC; 2022 pembrolizumab second-line established; 2019 enfortumab vedotin approved; BCG standard for NMIBC since 1990
