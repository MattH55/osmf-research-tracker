Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Acute insomnia (<3 months): 50-60% resolve spontaneously; chronic insomnia: only 20-25% spontaneous remission at 1 year; persistent insomnia in 50-75% without treatment
Best-intervention remission
Cognitive Behavioural Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I): 70-80% response rate; 40-60% remission (normalised sleep); durable at 6-24 months post-treatment; superior to sleep medication in long-term outcomes; digital CBT-I (Sleepio, Somryst) equivalent efficacy to in-person

Gap size
Very large — CBT-I is guideline-recommended as first-line but almost universally unavailable; medication is default
Primary barrier
access/framing
Barrier detail: AASM and most sleep guidelines recommend CBT-I as first-line over medication for chronic insomnia; fewer than 1% of insomnia sufferers have access to a CBT-I trained therapist; digital CBT-I exists and works but is rarely prescribed; benzodiazepines and z-drugs continue to dominate prescribing despite guidelines against long-term use

Last SoC change: 2022 — FDA approved daridorexant (Quviviq — orexin receptor antagonist); 2019 lemborexant; 2014 suvorexant (first orexin antagonist); z-drugs (zopiclone, zolpidem) since 1990s; benzodiazepines for sleep since 1960s
