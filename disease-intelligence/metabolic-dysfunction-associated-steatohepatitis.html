Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
MASH (formerly NASH) with fibrosis progresses to cirrhosis in ~20% over 10 years on standard care (which until 2024 was lifestyle modification only); fibrosis regression occurs spontaneously in ~25% over 5 years with sustained weight loss
Best-intervention remission
Resmetirom (Rezdiffra) — first FDA-approved pharmacotherapy for MASH (March 2024): 26-30% histological resolution at 52 weeks vs 10% placebo; bariatric surgery achieves MASH resolution in 85% and fibrosis regression in 35% at 5 years; >7-10% weight loss resolves MASH in ~50%

Gap size
Large — first drug only just approved in 2024 after decades of failed trials; practice still dominated by lifestyle advice alone
Primary barrier
biological (historical) + access (current)
Barrier detail: Until March 2024, there was literally no approved pharmacotherapy for MASH despite it being a leading cause of cirrhosis and liver transplant; the field repeatedly failed Phase 3 (obeticholic acid, elafibranor, selonsertib, cenicriviroc — all negative); resmetirom is a genuine breakthrough but access and prescribing uptake are still ramping up

Last SoC change: March 2024 — resmetirom (Rezdiffra) FDA approved — the first pharmacotherapy for MASH; prior standard was weight loss and lifestyle only since MASH was described in 1980
