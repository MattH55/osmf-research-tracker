Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive; GFR decline averages 4-5 mL/min/year in Mayo Class 1C-1E; renal failure by 60-70 years in 50% without treatment; spontaneous stabilisation rare
Best-intervention remission
Tolvaptan (Jynarque): slows GFR decline by 35% and total kidney volume growth by 49% (TEMPO 3:4, REPRISE); dietary protein restriction + hydration: modest benefit; SGLT2 inhibitors — early signal in PKD trials
Drug-free remission
—
Relapse after remission
—
Chronicity
—
Gap size
Moderate — tolvaptan is available and effective but hepatotoxicity risk limits use; access barriers (REMS programme in US) reduce prescribing
Primary barrier
access/practice
Barrier detail: Tolvaptan REMS programme (liver toxicity monitoring) creates prescribing complexity; only patients with rapidly progressive disease (Mayo Class 1C-1E) are eligible by guideline; most patients remain on supportive care without disease-modifying therapy

Last SoC change: 2018 — tolvaptan FDA approved for ADPKD; TEMPO 3:4 published 2012; mTOR inhibitors (sirolimus, everolimus) tried and failed in RCTs 2010; supportive care unchanged for decades until tolvaptan
