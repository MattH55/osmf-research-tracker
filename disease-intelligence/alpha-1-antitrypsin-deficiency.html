Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Progressive lung and liver disease; ZZ genotype: FEV1 decline accelerated (80-100 mL/year vs 30 mL/year in non-smokers); liver cirrhosis progressive; no spontaneous resolution without augmentation therapy
Best-intervention remission
Alpha-1 augmentation therapy (IV human AAT): slows CT-measured emphysema progression by 34% (RAPID trial); smoking cessation: most important modifiable factor (reduces FEV1 decline to near-normal rate); fazirsiran (RNAi targeting hepatic AAT production): 83% reduction in Z-AAT liver accumulation

Gap size
Very large — only 5-10% of AATD individuals are diagnosed; augmentation therapy reduces CT progression but functional benefit debated; liver disease has no approved disease-modifying therapy
Primary barrier
access + practice (underdiagnosis)
Barrier detail: AATD is misdiagnosed as COPD or asthma for a mean of 5-7 years; spirometry for COPD should trigger AATD testing but this is rarely done; fazirsiran (RNAi) reduces hepatic Z-protein accumulation — Phase 3 underway for liver disease; augmentation therapy is IV every 4 weeks lifelong (~$100K/year)

Last SoC change: 2022 — Phase 3 ATIXA trial of fazirsiran ongoing; RAPID trial results (2015) established augmentation therapy; IV augmentation therapy approved 1987; no major change in lung AATD management for 35 years
