Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Without anticoagulation: DVT progresses to PE in 30-50%; massive PE has 30%+ mortality without treatment; spontaneous resolution of small PE in 30-40% but recurrence risk high
Best-intervention remission
DOACs (rivaroxaban, apixaban): equal or superior efficacy to warfarin with 50% reduction in major bleeding; catheter-directed thrombolysis for large DVT: rapid thrombus resolution but unclear long-term benefit on PTS (ATTRACT trial); acute massive PE with hemodynamic compromise: systemic thrombolysis reduces mortality 55-65%

Gap size
Moderate — DOACs have largely replaced warfarin; remaining gap is in chronic anticoagulation decision-making and post-VTE care
Primary barrier
practice
Barrier detail: DOAC superiority is established but warfarin persistence remains common in patients with mechanical valves (where DOACs are contraindicated) and in some LMIC settings; duration of anticoagulation after unprovoked VTE is an individualised decision poorly standardised; post-thrombotic syndrome (PTS) prevention with compression is rarely implemented

Last SoC change: 2023 — betrixaban/edoxaban expanding in specific populations; 2020 Andexanet alfa (reversal agent for FXa inhibitors) established; DOACs first-line since 2012-2014 (EINSTEIN, AMPLIFY trials); warfarin standard since 1954
