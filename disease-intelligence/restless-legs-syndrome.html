Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Primary RLS is chronic; spontaneous remission in mild cases ~30-40% over 5 years; pregnancy-induced RLS remits post-delivery; augmentation on dopaminergic drugs worsens symptoms in 60-80% at 10 years
Best-intervention remission
Iron IV supplementation in iron-deficient RLS: 57% responders vs 35% placebo (ferric carboxymaltose — Phase 3 positive); alpha-2-delta ligands (gabapentin enacarbil, pregabalin): 53-55% RLS responders; dopamine agonists (pramipexole, ropinirole): 70-80% initial response but augmentation in 60-80% at 10 years

Gap size
Moderate — iron deficiency treatment is underused as primary intervention; dopamine agonists chronically overused despite augmentation risk
Primary barrier
framing/practice
Barrier detail: IV iron therapy for iron-deficient RLS (<50 µg/L ferritin) is highly effective and underused; dopamine agonists are first-line in most practices despite augmentation being essentially inevitable in long-term use; augmentation causes 24-hour symptoms worse than original disease

Last SoC change: 2023 — updated AAN/AASM guidelines recommending alpha-2-delta ligands over dopamine agonists as first-line (augmentation concern); ferric carboxymaltose approved for iron-deficient RLS; dopamine agonists (ropinirole, pramipexole) as first-line since 2006
