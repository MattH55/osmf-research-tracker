Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
PV/ET/MF: no spontaneous remission; progressive disorders with risk of transformation to MF (PV/ET) and blast phase (all); median survival PV >20 years, MF 5-7 years
Best-intervention remission
JAK2 inhibitor (ruxolitinib) for MF/PV: 35-42% spleen volume reduction, significant symptom improvement; ropeginterferon alfa-2b: superior complete haematological response vs hydroxyurea (PROUD-PV); allogeneic HSCT (MF): curative in 30-60% of transplant-eligible patients
Drug-free remission
—
Relapse after remission
—
Chronicity
—
Gap size
Moderate — ruxolitinib is established but HSCT underused in eligible MF patients; ropeginterferon is superior in PV but access varies
Primary barrier
access/practice
Barrier detail: Hydroxyurea remains the most common cytoreductive agent for PV/ET despite ropeginterferon demonstrating superior molecular response; HSCT for MF is underutilised in patients who could benefit; JAK2 mutation molecular monitoring not performed systematically

Last SoC change: 2022 — ropeginterferon alfa-2b (Besremi) FDA approved for PV; 2019 fedratinib (JAK2/FLT3) for MF; pacritinib 2022 for thrombocytopenic MF; ruxolitinib 2011 for MF, 2014 for PV; hydroxyurea standard since 1960s
