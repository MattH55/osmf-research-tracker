Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Low-grade (Gleason 6/Grade Group 1): active surveillance appropriate in 40-50% — many will never need treatment; high-grade: lethal without treatment within 5-10 years
Best-intervention remission
Localised: surgery (radical prostatectomy) or radiotherapy — 90-95% disease-specific survival at 10 years; mCSPC: darolutamide + docetaxel + ADT (ARASENS): 32% OS improvement; enzalutamide/abiraterone + ADT as standard 2019-2021; PARP inhibitors for BRCA-mutant: olaparib — 34% OS benefit (PROfound)

Primary barrier
practice
Barrier detail: Active surveillance in low-risk PCa is guideline-endorsed but only 50-60% of eligible patients are managed this way (radical treatment used unnecessarily with significant sexual/urinary side effects); intensified ADT combinations are standard of care for mHSPC but ADT monotherapy still prescribed

Last SoC change: 2022-2023 — darolutamide + ADT + docetaxel triplet; PARP inhibitors for HRR-mutant mCRPC; 2021 lutetium-177-PSMA-617 FDA approved; 2019 first-line enzalutamide/abiraterone for mHSPC; PSA testing debate ongoing
