Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous reversion to sinus rhythm occurs in ~50% of paroxysmal AF within 24-48 hours; persistent AF almost never self-terminates; rate-control (the dominant strategy) by definition keeps the patient in AF
Best-intervention remission
Catheter ablation achieves freedom from AF in ~70-80% at 1 year (paroxysmal), 50-60% (persistent); ablation superior to antiarrhythmic drugs (52% vs 71% AF recurrence, CABANA trial); EAST-AFNET 4 showed early rhythm control reduced major adverse CV events vs rate control

Primary barrier
practice
Barrier detail: Guidelines now support earlier rhythm control and ablation, but rate control with lifelong anticoagulation remains the dominant practice pattern; AF management is described as 'moving ahead of established guidelines' per ESC 2023

Last SoC change: 2023 — ESC guidelines restructured with new AF management acronym (AF-CARE); recommendation for early rhythm control strengthened (EAST-AFNET 4 data); 2014 ACC/AHA rate-vs-rhythm neutral stance increasingly outdated; first-line rate control drugs (beta-blockers, digoxin) unchanged since 1990s
