Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous fluctuation common; resolution in ~25% at 1 year; bladder training alone achieves 50-80% improvement in urgency frequency; OAB worsens with age without intervention
Best-intervention remission
Bladder training + pelvic floor exercises: 60-80% improvement (first-line guideline recommendation); anticholinergics/beta-3 agonist (mirabegron): 30-60% significant improvement; botulinum toxin A (OnabotulinumtoxinA, Botox): 30-50% complete continence at 12 weeks; sacral neuromodulation (InterStim): 72% success at 5 years

Gap size
Large — most patients receive pharmacotherapy as first-line without adequate behavioural therapy trial; anticholinergics overused in elderly
Primary barrier
framing/practice
Barrier detail: Behavioural interventions (pelvic floor training, bladder diary, timed voiding) are first-line by all guidelines but most patients receive anticholinergics immediately; anticholinergic cognitive burden in elderly patients is significant (dementia risk association with chronic use — JAMA Intern Med 2019); mirabegron has better cognitive safety profile but is underused relative to older anticholinergics

Last SoC change: 2021 — AUA/SUFU OAB guideline updated; implantable tibial nerve stimulator (NURO) added 2019; onabotulinumtoxinA for OAB since 2013; mirabegron (Myrbetriq) 2012; anticholinergics (oxybutynin) since 1975
