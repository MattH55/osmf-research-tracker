Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
30-40% of people with AUD achieve sustained abstinence without formal treatment at 1 year (natural recovery); but functional impairment, liver damage, and mortality risk accumulate; relapse in 50-60% within 1 year even after treatment
Best-intervention remission
Naltrexone (COMBINE trial): 23% abstinence rate vs 14% placebo at 16 weeks; combined pharmacotherapy + CBT: best outcomes (~30-40% sustained abstinence at 1 year); acamprosate: reduces relapse risk 14% (meta-analysis); liver cirrhosis reversal with abstinence: fibrosis regression in 30-40% at 5 years

Gap size

Primary barrier
access/framing
Barrier detail: AUD is massively undertreated; only 5.7% of people with AUD in the US received any treatment in 2019 (SAMHSA); naltrexone and acamprosate are dramatically underprescribed (<1% of AUD patients); 12-step/abstinence framing excludes patients seeking harm reduction; primary care providers rarely screen or treat

Last SoC change: 2023 — GLP-1 agonists showing alcohol craving reduction (semaglutide reducing alcohol use in trials); 2006 acamprosate approved; naltrexone monthly injectable (Vivitrol) since 2006; naltrexone oral since 1994; disulfiram since 1951; essentially unchanged core pharmacotherapy for 30 years
