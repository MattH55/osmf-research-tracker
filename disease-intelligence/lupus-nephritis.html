Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous remission essentially zero in class III/IV LN; progression to ESRD in 10-20% at 10 years on standard therapy; renal flares in 30-50% of patients achieving initial response
Best-intervention remission
Voclosporin + MMF + low-dose OCS: 40.8% complete renal response at 52 weeks (AURORA 1); belimumab + SOC: 43% primary efficacy renal response (BLISS-LN); obinutuzumab + MMF + SOC: superior histological response vs rituximab (NOBILITY)

Gap size
Moderate — better outcomes available with triple therapy but access and racial disparities limit implementation
Primary barrier
access/practice
Barrier detail: Black and Hispanic patients have significantly worse LN outcomes and are underrepresented in landmark trials; voclosporin and belimumab add significant cost; cyclophosphamide still used in some settings where MMF is equally effective in non-Black patients (Euro-Lupus low-dose equivalent); racial disparities in specialist access

Last SoC change: 2021 — voclosporin (Lupkynis) approved; belimumab IV added for LN (BLISS-LN); MMF established as standard for induction since ACR 2012 guidelines; cyclophosphamide from 1986 (NIH protocol)
