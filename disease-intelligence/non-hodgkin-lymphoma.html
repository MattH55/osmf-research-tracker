Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Indolent NHL (follicular): spontaneous remission in 20-30% of early-stage, allowing watch-and-wait; aggressive NHL (DLBCL): rapidly lethal without treatment in weeks-months
Best-intervention remission
DLBCL with R-CHOP: 60-70% cure rate; Pola-R-CHP (polatuzumab): 27% improvement in 2-year PFS (POLARIX); DLBCL relapsed: axicabtagene ciloleucel (axi-cel) CAR-T: 40-50% long-term remission; follicular lymphoma: obinutuzumab + chemotherapy 85-90% response, durable
Drug-free remission
—
Relapse after remission
—
Chronicity
—
Gap size
Variable by subtype; DLBCL has genuinely curative potential; indolent NHL remains manageable but generally incurable with current therapy
Primary barrier
access (CAR-T) + biological (indolent NHL)
Barrier detail: CAR-T therapy is transformative in relapsed DLBCL but manufacturing time (2-3 weeks), cost ($400K+), and centre requirements limit access; bispecific antibodies (epcoritamab, glofitamab) offer off-the-shelf alternative

Last SoC change: 2022-2023 — bispecific antibodies (epcoritamab, glofitamab) approved for relapsed DLBCL; CAR-T as second-line (ZUMA-7, TRANSFORM) since 2022; Pola-R-CHP first-line 2023; R-CHOP since 2000 (CHOP since 1970s)
