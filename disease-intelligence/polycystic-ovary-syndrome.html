Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
PCOS is a lifelong endocrine disorder; complete spontaneous resolution rare; some phenotypic improvement with menopause; fertility returns in 50-70% with ovulation induction; metabolic features persist
Best-intervention remission
Weight loss of 5-10% restores ovulation in 55-90% of anovulatory PCOS women; metformin restores ovulation in 30-50%; letrozole (ovulation induction): 27% live birth rate per cycle (PPCOS II); lifestyle modification: 50-80% improvement in menstrual regularity

Gap size
Large — lifestyle modification and metformin are evidence-based first-line but most patients receive OCPs (treating symptoms only, not the underlying metabolic disorder)
Primary barrier
framing/practice
Barrier detail: OCP (oral contraceptive pill) is most commonly prescribed for PCOS, treating symptoms (irregular periods, hirsutism) without addressing the underlying insulin resistance and metabolic risk; OCP does not treat infertility and may worsen insulin sensitivity; the PCOS field has lacked a clear pharmacotherapy standard

Last SoC change: 2023 — International PCOS Guideline updated; letrozole formally recommended over clomiphene for ovulation induction; metformin recommended as first-line metabolic treatment; GLP-1 agonists emerging as adjunct; spironolactone for hyperandrogenism; first guidelines were 2011 (Endocrine Society)
