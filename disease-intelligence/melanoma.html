Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous regression documented in ~0.4% of metastatic melanoma cases; early-stage surgical resection: 90%+ 5-year survival for Stage I; Stage IV (metastatic): historically <10% 5-year survival
Best-intervention remission
Stage IV with PD-1/CTLA-4 combination (nivolumab + ipilimumab): 52% 5-year OS (CheckMate-067); BRAF V600E + MEK inhibition (dabrafenib + trametinib): 34% 5-year OS; pembrolizumab adjuvant Stage III: 58.4% vs 45.9% RFS at 5 years; TIL therapy (Amtagvi): 31.5% ORR in heavily pretreated patients

Gap size
Moderate — genuine cures are now achievable for many Stage IV melanoma patients; the question is maximising who can access these outcomes
Primary barrier
access
Barrier detail: Immunotherapy combination (nivolumab + ipilimumab) achieves durable responses in ~25-30% of Stage IV patients (functional cure); BRAF-targeted therapy is highly effective in BRAF V600E (50% of melanomas) but resistance develops in most; tumour-infiltrating lymphocyte (TIL) therapy (Amtagvi, 2024) approved — cell manufacturing is complex

Last SoC change: 2024 — Amtagvi (lifileucel, TIL therapy) FDA approved — first tumour-infiltrating lymphocyte cell therapy approved for any cancer; 2020 adjuvant pembrolizumab Stage IIB-III; 2011 ipilimumab (first checkpoint inhibitor approved for any cancer); PD-1 inhibitors since 2014
