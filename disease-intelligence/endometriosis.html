Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Endometriosis lesions may partially regress after menopause; spontaneous resolution of symptoms rare; recurrence rate after surgery 20-40% at 2 years and 50-60% at 5 years
Best-intervention remission
Excision surgery achieves pain relief in 60-80% at 6 months but 50% recurrence at 5 years; GnRH agonists + add-back therapy: 70-90% pain reduction but oestrogen-deprivation side effects; combined OCP: 50-60% pain reduction; elagolix (Orilissa): 43% reduction in dysmenorrhoea vs placebo (ELARIS EM-I)

Gap size
Very large — diagnostic delay averages 7-10 years from symptom onset; no disease-modifying treatment; high recurrence after surgery
Primary barrier
biological + practice
Barrier detail: Diagnostic delay of 7-10 years is the largest modifiable factor; endometriosis dismissed as 'normal period pain'; no non-surgical diagnostic test; all medical treatments are suppressive not curative; recurrence after surgery is universal without continued medical suppression

Last SoC change: 2023 — linzagolix (Yselty, GnRH antagonist) approved in Europe; 2021 relugolix combination (Myfembree) FDA approved; elagolix (Orilissa) 2018; GnRH agonists standard since 1980s; excision surgery approach unchanged for decades
