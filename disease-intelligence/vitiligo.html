Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Spontaneous complete repigmentation extremely rare (<1%); partial spontaneous repigmentation possible in small lesions; progressive without treatment in most patients
Best-intervention remission
Ruxolitinib cream (Opzelura): 29.9% F-VASI75 (75% facial repigmentation) at 24 weeks vs 7.5% vehicle; NB-UVB phototherapy: 75%+ repigmentation in 30-40% of patients at 12 months; excimer laser for localised vitiligo: 75%+ repigmentation in 50-70%; combination ruxolitinib + NB-UVB: superior to either alone

Gap size
Large — first topical treatment only approved 2022; NB-UVB is effective but access to specialised units is limited
Primary barrier
access
Barrier detail: Prior to ruxolitinib cream (2022), there was no FDA-approved pharmacotherapy for vitiligo; NB-UVB is effective but requires 2-3x/week sessions for months and is only available in dermatology/phototherapy units; cosmetic dismissal of vitiligo as 'not a medical disease' has delayed research and treatment access

Last SoC change: 2022 — ruxolitinib cream (Opzelura) FDA approved for non-segmental vitiligo — first ever approved pharmacotherapy; prior standard was off-label topical corticosteroids and NB-UVB
