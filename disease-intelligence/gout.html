Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Acute flares self-resolve in 7-14 days without treatment; but urate crystal deposition continues; only 10-15% achieve serum urate <360 µmol/L spontaneously without ULT; progressive tophus formation and joint damage in untreated patients
Best-intervention remission
Allopurinol/febuxostat: serum urate target (<360 µmol/L) achieved in 60-70% if adequately dosed; treat-to-target with regular monitoring: 80-90% flare-free at 2 years; pegloticase: 40% complete tophus resolution at 6 months in refractory patients

Gap size
Very large — gout is one of the most undertreated diseases in medicine despite highly effective therapy
Primary barrier
framing/practice
Barrier detail: Gout is widely (incorrectly) perceived as a diet-only disease by patients and many clinicians; ULT (urate-lowering therapy) is initiated in only 30-40% of eligible patients; of those who start ULT, only 30-40% reach serum urate target because doses are not escalated; gout management is the single most guideline-discordant area in rheumatology

Last SoC change: 2020 — ACR guidelines recommenced treat-to-target with regular dose titration of allopurinol; 2021 canakinumab guideline endorsement for frequent flare prevention; febuxostat CV safety concern (Black Box warning 2019 based on CARES trial); allopurinol standard since 1966
