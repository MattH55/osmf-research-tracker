What Qualifies as "Unmet Medical Need"?

These two filtering criteria are often confused. Here's the key distinction:

❌ Too Strict: "Zero Phase 4 Drugs"

Diseases with absolutely no approved drugs, even for symptom management.

Examples:

  • Mastocytosis KIT D816V (no treatments)
  • Sjögren's Syndrome (no treatments)
Count: 3 diseases

✓ Correct: "No Indication-Specific Approval"

Diseases with generic symptom drugs, but NO disease-modifying therapy approved for THAT disease.

Examples:

  • ME/CFS: Has antidepressants, stimulants → treat symptoms only
  • Long COVID: Has anticoagulants for other uses → no approved SARS recovery therapy
  • Alzheimer's: 233 approved drugs → ZERO for cognitive decline in Alzheimer's
Count: 82 diseases (THIS registry)
Legal Standard: 21 U.S.C. § 360bbb-0a (federal) and Montana SB 535 both require lack of "adequate approved therapy" for the specific disease. A drug approved for hypertension doesn't count as therapy for ME/CFS, even though it's Phase 4. Right-to-try eligibility is about disease-modifying therapy for THIS indication.
