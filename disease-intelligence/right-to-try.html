Unmet Medical Needs

Definition: Diseases with no FDA-approved (Phase 4) therapeutic agents in the RepurpOS database. These represent true unmet medical needs where right-to-try and compassionate use frameworks may apply.

Status: This registry identifies 82 diseases from 106 condition profiles without indication-specific FDA approval. Diseases are filtered by presence of Phase 4 (approved) agents; those with Phase 1-3 investigational agents are highlighted as having promising candidates for right-to-try consideration.

Legal Framework: Federal right-to-try eligibility (21 U.S.C. § 360bbb-0a) and Montana SB 535 access. Critical distinctions: drug-level eligibility (not indication-level), ex-US approval non-disqualification, and Phase 1 population risk calibration for dose escalation trials in oncology.

Data Sources: Open Targets Platform (gene-to-drug mapping), ClinicalTrials.gov, FDA Orange/Purple Books, and OSMF curation. Phase 1-3 agents are investigational—not licensed therapy.
