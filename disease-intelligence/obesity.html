Remission & chronicity

Spontaneous remission
Sustained weight loss of >5% without intervention: ~5-10% at 2 years; weight regain almost universal after behavioural intervention alone (95% return to within 5% of starting weight at 5 years on lifestyle alone)
Best-intervention remission
GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide 2.4mg): 15-17% mean weight loss at 68 weeks (STEP 1); tirzepatide (dual GIP/GLP-1): 20-22% at 72 weeks (SURMOUNT-1); bariatric surgery: 25-35% sustained weight loss at 10 years with metabolic syndrome resolution

Gap size
Very large — effective pharmacotherapy now exists but is massively under-prescribed; access is the primary barrier
Primary barrier
access/framing
Barrier detail: Obesity is still widely treated as a lifestyle choice rather than a chronic disease by payers; GLP-1 medications (semaglutide, tirzepatide) cost $1,000-1,300/month and are excluded from most Medicare coverage; stigma in clinical settings reduces treatment-seeking; bariatric surgery used in <1% of eligible patients

Last SoC change: 2023 — tirzepatide (Zepbound) FDA approved for obesity; 2021 semaglutide (Wegovy) approved; 2024 semaglutide approved for CV risk reduction in obesity without diabetes (SELECT trial: 20% CV event reduction); prior obesity medications (orlistat, phentermine) essentially unchanged since 1990s-2000s
