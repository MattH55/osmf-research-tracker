## Overview

Retatrutide (LY3437943) is a triple GLP-1/GIP/GCG receptor agonist in Phase 3 development by Eli Lilly. It is the first compound combining agonism of all three incretin/metabolic hormones.

**Mechanism:** 
- **GLP-1 agonism:** glucose-dependent insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, delayed gastric emptying, appetite suppression
- **GIP agonism:** synergistic insulin potentiation, energy expenditure, appetite suppression
- **GCG agonism** (novel): increased hepatic glucose output (glucose-dependent), increased lipolysis, metabolic rate elevation, potential direct weight-loss signal via hypothalamic GCG receptor activation

Theoretically, triple agonism should produce superior glucose control and weight loss vs. dual agonists (tirzepatide), which has been observed in early Phase 3 data.

## Evidence Summary

### Type 2 Diabetes (E2)
**TRANSCEND-T2D-1** (completed, N=914): Triple-dose escalation design. HbA1c reductions at highest doses: 1.9–2.4% from baseline vs. 0.4% placebo (p<0.001). Dose-dependent response; 8 mg dose shows 2.4% reduction (comparable or superior to tirzepatide 15 mg).

### Chronic Weight Management (E2)
**TRIUMPH-1** (completed, N=500+): Weight loss 21–22% from baseline at highest doses vs. 3% placebo (p<0.001). Comparable to tirzepatide 15 mg (21–22%). 

Early indirect comparison data (not head-to-head): retatrutide 8 mg ≈ tirzepatide 15 mg for weight loss; retatrutide superior for HbA1c reduction in T2DM.

### Cardiovascular Risk Reduction (E4)
**No human data.** Dedicated CVOT registered (N~5000) but not yet completed. Expected 2027–2028. Mechanism speculated:
- Weight loss + glycemic control (known cardiovascular benefit)
- GCG agonism: increased metabolic rate, hepatic lipid reduction (speculative; never tested in humans at scale)
- Direct GCG-mediated cardioprotection (unknown; no prior human cardiovascular trials of GCG agonists)

## Safety Profile (S3 — Moderate Safety Data, Novel Mechanism)

**Adverse Events (>5% vs. placebo):**
- Nausea (50–60%, dose-limiting; higher than tirzepatide)
- Vomiting (20–30%)
- Diarrhea (30–40%)
- Constipation (15–20%)
- Abdominal pain (15–20%)

**Serious/Notable:**
- **Gallbladder-related events (EMERGENT SIGNAL):** TRANSCEND-T2D-1 showed increased cholecystectomy and cholelithiasis-related adverse events. Mechanism: GCG agonism stimulates gallbladder emptying; long-term biliary stone formation unknown. ~1–2% excess gallbladder events vs. placebo.
- Acute kidney injury (class signal; GCG-mediated lipolysis may compound diuretic effect)
- Pancreatitis (not observed Phase 3, but GLP-1 class signal + GCG unknown mechanism)
- GI intolerance (highest among all GLP-1/GIP/GCG agonists; ~20% discontinue due to nausea)

## What Is Unknown (Critical Gaps)

1. **⚠️ Long-term safety (>2 years).** Longest trial arm: 2 years. Full Phase 3 data: 2 years. Long-term effects of sustained triple agonism unknown.

2. **⚠️ GCG agonism–specific toxicity.** This is the first widely-dosed GCG agonist in humans. GCG signaling touches hepatic metabolism, pancreatic α-cell function, and lipolysis. Unknown off-target effects.

3. **⚠️ Gallbladder/biliary disease trajectory.** Emergent signal in TRANSCEND-T2D-1. Incidence may increase with years of use. Unknown if excess cholecystectomy is reversible or indicates underlying pathology.

4. **⚠️ Cardiovascular mechanism & outcomes.** Zero human cardiovascular trial data. CVOT expected 2027–2028. Unknown if triple agonism confers cardiovascular benefit beyond weight loss and glycemic control.

5. **⚠️ Pancreatic safety with GLP-1 + GCG.** No pancreatitis cases Phase 3. GLP-1 class signal + GCG agonism (activates pancreatic α-cells, increases glucagon): unknown if combination amplifies pancreatitis risk.

6. **⚠️ Drug interactions.** Limited Phase 3 interaction data. GCG agonism affects hepatic metabolism; unknown interactions with CYP450 substrates, other gluconeogenic agents.

7. **⚠️ Tolerability beyond 2 years.** High discontinuation rate due to nausea (20%). Long-term adherence & sustained weight loss unknown.

8. **⚠️ Discontinuation/rebound effects.** Rapid cessation of powerful appetite suppressant; rebound appetite, mood changes, weight regain unknown. No long-term off-drug data.
