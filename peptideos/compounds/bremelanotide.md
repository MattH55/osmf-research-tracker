# Bremelanotide (Vyleesi)

## Overview

Bremelanotide is an alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (alpha-MSH) analog, approved for hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD) in premenopausal women. It is the first FDA-approved melanocortin receptor agonist for sexual dysfunction.

**Mechanism:** MC1R and MC4R agonism → activation of hypothalamic neurons regulating sexual desire and arousal. Mechanism of action distinct from phosphodiesterase inhibitors (which address vascular flow).

## Indications & Approvals

| Indication | Formulation | Approval (Year) | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| HSDD (premenopausal women) | Vyleesi (subcutaneous) | 2019 | Active |

## Evidence Summary

### HSDD Treatment (E1)
RECONNECT trial (N=1247): 38% increase in satisfying sexual events vs. 20% placebo (NNT ~6). Effect onset within 30 minutes of injection. Maintained at 1-year open-label extension.

## Safety Profile (S3)

**Adverse Events:**
- Nausea (20%, dose-limiting)
- Headache (8%)
- Flushing (5%)
- Hypertension (modest, 3–5 mmHg increase)

**Serious:**
- Melanoma risk (black box warning; theoretical, based on melanocyte-stimulating mechanism; no cases in trials)

## What Is Unknown

1. **Long-term melanoma risk.** Trials max 1 year; long-term monitoring recommended
2. **Efficacy in men.** Mechanism suggests potential; not tested for male ED
3. **Reversibility.** Whether libido reverts post-discontinuation
